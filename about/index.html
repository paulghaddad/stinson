About Us

Bob Stinson, Psy.D., J.D., LICDC-CS, ABPP, is a board certified forensic psychologist, attorney at law, and licensed independent chemical dependency counselor – clinical supervisor. He obtained his doctorate degree in clinical psychology from Wright State University’s School of Professional Psychology and his law degree from Capital University Law School. Dr. Stinson is listed in the National Register of Health Service Providers in Psychology. He is a Fellow of the American Academy of Forensic Psychology (AAFP) and a Diplomate in forensic psychology with the American Board of Professional Psychology (ABPP). He has full clinical and special forensic hospital privileges at Twin Valley Behavioral Healthcare in Columbus, Ohio, where he also Chairs the hospital’s ethics committee. He is an Adjunct Professor of Clinical Psychology at The Ohio State University and a Clinical Associate Professor at Wright State University’s School of Professional Psychology. Dr. Stinson holds membership in the American Psychological Association (and several of its divisions), the Ohio Psychological Association (where he serves on the Board of Directors as the Chair of the Ethics Committee), and he is a Past-President of the Central Ohio Psychological Association (COPA). He is also a member of the Ohio State Bar Association. He has lectured throughout the country on mental health law and ethics. In addition to working at Twin Valley, Dr. Stinson has a general law practice, though he focuses on Administrative Law and disciplinary actions professionals face from their regulatory boards; and he is President of Stinson & Associates, Inc., providing consultations and evaluations, specializing in clinical and forensic psychology.

Jennifer M. Kutys, Psy.D., is an independently licensed psychologist. Dr. Kutys specializes in Clinical and Forensic Psychology. She obtained her Bachelor’s degree in Psychology and Criminology at The Ohio State University in Columbus, Ohio. She obtained her Master’s and Doctorate degrees in Clinical Psychology from Wright State University, School of Professional Psychology. Dr. Kutys completed a clinical and forensic psychology internship with the U.S. Department of Justice, Federal Bureau of Prisons in Rochester, Minnesota. Dr. Kutys then completed post-doctoral and post-licensure training in the specialty area of Forensic Psychology. She has practiced psychology in both the state and federal criminal justice systems. Throughout her education and training, Dr. Kutys has worked in outpatient treatment centers, inpatient forensic hospitals, private practices, and in local, state, and federal correctional centers. She has specialized skill, knowledge, education, experience, and training in the areas of forensic psychology, clinical psychology, severe mental illness, personality disorders, substance abuse disorders, inpatient treatment and evaluations, disability determination, and fitness for duty evaluations. Dr. Kutys works with adolescents, adults, and geriatric patients. Her private practice consists largely of completing evaluations and providing consultations. She has taught graduate-level students at Wright State University and provides clinical supervision to psychologists in training. She has a variety of professional experiences that reflect the breadth and depth of her training and expertise. She maintains her level of expertise through continued education, consultation, and her association with a number of professional organizations.
